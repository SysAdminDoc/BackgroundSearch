BackgroundSearch — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-20

Data Collection

BackgroundSearch does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data, browsing history, search queries, or analytics.

Permissions

  • tabs / tabGroups — Used to open search results in background tabs and group them. No tab URLs are logged or transmitted.
  • contextMenus — Used to create right-click search engine menu items.
  • storage — Used to save your settings (enabled engines, site rules, theme). Data stays in Chrome's built-in sync storage and is never sent to any external server.
  • scripting — Used to track Shift key state and middle-click capture on pages.

Third-Party Services

When you search, your query is sent directly to the search engine you selected (Google, Bing, etc.) via a standard browser navigation. BackgroundSearch does not proxy, intercept, or modify your search traffic.

Local Storage

Your configuration (enabled engines, custom engines, groups, site rules, theme) is stored in chrome.storage.sync and optionally chrome.storage.local. This data syncs across your Chrome instances via your Google account if Chrome sync is enabled. BackgroundSearch has no access to your Google account.

Open Source

BackgroundSearch is open source under the MIT License. You can inspect the full source code at github.com/SysAdminDoc/BackgroundSearch.

Contact

For questions about this privacy policy, open an issue on the GitHub repository.
